<p><p>Metallised film refers to a thin layer of metal, usually aluminum, that is applied to a plastic film substrate. This process enhances the film's barrier properties, making it impermeable to moisture, gases, odor, and light. Metallised films are widely used in various industries such as packaging, automotive, electronics, and construction.</p><p>The future outlook of the metallised film market appears promising. The increasing demand for flexible packaging solutions across different industries is a significant driver for the market. Additionally, the rising demand for eco-friendly and sustainable packaging materials is expected to boost the market further. Metallised films are recyclable and contribute to reducing the overall waste generated by the packaging industry.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in technology are leading to the development of high-performance metallised films with improved barrier properties. The use of metallised films in the solar industry for reflective and heat-protective purposes is also gaining traction.</p><p>However, the current outlook of the metallised film market is slightly hampered by the increasing popularity of alternatives such as metallised paper and bio-based films.
