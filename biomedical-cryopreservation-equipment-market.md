<p><p>Biomedical cryopreservation equipment refers to a range of medical devices used to store and preserve biological materials at specific temperatures. The market includes various types of equipment to cater to different temperature requirements. Medical refrigerators maintain temperatures between 2℃-8℃, ideal for storing vaccines and medications. Blood refrigerators keep blood at a precise temperature of 4℃±1℃. Medical freezers have a temperature range of -10℃ to -25℃, suitable for preserving sensitive biological samples. Medical cryopreservation boxes offer a wider temperature range of -10℃ to -50℃. Medical cryogenic storage boxes provide even lower temperatures, ranging from -50℃ to -150℃. Liquid nitrogen tanks are used to achieve extremely low temperatures between -150℃ and -196℃. Lastly, there are also other types of biomedical cryopreservation equipment available in the market to fulfill specific temperature requirements.</p></p>
