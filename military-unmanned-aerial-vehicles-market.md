<p><p>Military Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) can be classified into two main types: Fixed Wing and Rotary Wing. Fixed Wing UAVs are similar to traditional airplanes, using wings to generate lift and propulsion to move forward. They have longer flight endurance and higher speeds, making them ideal for long-range surveillance and strike missions. On the other hand, Rotary Wing UAVs, also known as drones or quadcopters, rely on rotating blades to achieve lift and maneuverability. They are more agile and adaptable, suitable for close-range surveillance, reconnaissance, and urban operations. Both types serve different military purposes based on their unique capabilities.</p></p>
