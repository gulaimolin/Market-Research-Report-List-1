<p><p>Gypsum is a naturally occurring mineral composed of calcium sulfate dihydrate. It is commonly used in the construction industry due to its fire-resistant and soundproofing properties. When gypsum is ground into a powder and mixed with water, it forms a paste that can be used to create drywall panels. Drywall, also known as plasterboard or wallboard, is made up of a gypsum core sandwiched between two sheets of paper. It is a widely used building material for interior walls and ceilings.</p><p>The future outlook for the gypsum and drywall market is highly promising.
