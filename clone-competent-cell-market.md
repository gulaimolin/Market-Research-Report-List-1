<p><p>Clone competent cells are commonly used in molecular biology research to introduce foreign DNA into bacterial cells. Chemically competent cells are created through a chemical treatment that increases the permeability of the cell membrane to DNA. Electrocompetent cells are created using an electrical pulse to create temporary pores in the cell membrane. Both chemically and electrocompetent cells are widely used in genetic engineering, gene cloning, and expression studies. Their availability in the market ensures researchers have various options for delivering DNA efficiently into bacterial cells, depending on their specific experimental requirements.</p></p>
