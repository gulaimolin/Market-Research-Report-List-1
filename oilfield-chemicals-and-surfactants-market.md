<p><p>Oilfield chemicals and surfactants are used in the petroleum industry to enhance the extraction and production of oil and gas. Anionic oilfield chemicals and surfactants are negatively charged and are used for various purposes such as clay stabilization, emulsification, and detergent action. Non-ionic oilfield chemicals and surfactants have no charge and are primarily used as emulsifiers and wetting agents. Cationic oilfield chemicals and surfactants have a positive charge and are used for flocculation and corrosion inhibition. Amphoteric oilfield chemicals and surfactants can carry both positive and negative charges, making them versatile for a variety of applications. Other types of oilfield chemicals and surfactants include foam control agents, defoamers, and biocides.</p></p>
