<p><p>Welded pipes, also known as welded steel pipes, are pipes formed by welding a steel plate or strip after it has been bent into a circular shape. The welding process involves applying heat and pressure to melt the edges of the strip or plate and fuse them together, creating a seamless and durable pipe.</p><p>The global welded pipes market is witnessing significant growth due to various factors such as the increasing demand for energy and infrastructure development projects, particularly in emerging economies. The use of welded pipes in applications such as oil and gas pipelines, water and sewage systems, construction, and automotive industries is driving market growth.</p><p>Additionally, the advantages of welded pipes over seamless pipes, such as lower cost and ease of production, are further contributing to market growth. Welded pipes are cost-effective, as the manufacturing process is simpler and requires less material compared to seamless pipes.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in the welding techniques used for manufacturing welded pipes have improved their quality and strength, making them suitable for various applications. For instance, advancements in automated welding processes have enhanced the productivity and efficiency of welded pipe manufacturing.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing investments in the oil and gas industry, especially in regions like North America and the Middle East, are creating lucrative opportunities for the welded pipes market. These regions have significant demand for pipelines, driving the growth of the market.</p><p>Overall, the welded pipes market is expected to witness steady growth during the forecast period, with a projected CAGR of 5.6%.
